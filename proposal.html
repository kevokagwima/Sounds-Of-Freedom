About the Project

The Sounds of Freedom is an audio film series inspired by the lives and experiences of the young men and women who fought for Kenya’s independence.

Through this story, we hope to bring Kenyans closer to their history by welcoming them to immersive experiences that allow them to come face to with the realities of what it took to liberate Kenya from British.

Freedom Festival 2023

From Black History Month all February to Madaraka celebrations in June, and Heroes Day on October 20th to Day on December 12th, we to invite people from all walks of life re-discover the Sounds of Freedom all year round!

Our interactive app will use geolocalisation 360 spatial sound mapping technology well as augmented and virtual reality to audiences to discover our stories unlocking episodes at the very locations historical events occurred. Imagine through a forest together with the same characters you're listening to! into caves and listening to the of the resistance! Maybe you'll even sent on a secret mission that will take to secret locations and listen to that shaped the course of history!

Event Objectives

1

To uncover hidden gems of history and heritage, and new ways to connect the past.

2

To explore the complex and evolving concept of patriotism in a contemporary society. We'll be inviting a diverse panel of experts and thought leaders to share their insights and engage in a dynamic discussion on this timely topic

3

To promote environmental conservation by replanting indigenous trees in open urban spaces and on the slopes of Mt. Kenya. Together we can nurture the roots of a greener, more sustainable world for generations to come.

4

Once you experience the stories, we will invite you to book clubs, debates, workshops, plays, and concerts that give you the full patriotic experience.
