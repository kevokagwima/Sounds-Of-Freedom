About the Project

The Sounds of Freedom is an audio film series inspired by the lives and experiences of the young men and women who fought for Kenya’s independence.

Through this story, we hope to bring Kenyans closer to their history by welcoming them to immersive experiences that allow them to come face to with the realities of what it took to liberate Kenya from British.

Freedom Festival 2023

From Black History Month all February to Madaraka celebrations in June, and Heroes Day on October 20th to Day on December 12th, we to invite people from all walks of life re-discover the Sounds of Freedom all year round!

Our interactive app will use geolocalisation 360 spatial sound mapping technology well as augmented and virtual reality to audiences to discover our stories unlocking episodes at the very locations historical events occurred. Imagine through a forest together with the same characters you're listening to! into caves and listening to the of the resistance! Maybe you'll even sent on a secret mission that will take to secret locations and listen to that shaped the course of history!

Event Objectives

1

To uncover hidden gems of history and heritage, and new ways to connect the past.

2

To explore the complex and evolving concept of patriotism in a contemporary society. We'll be inviting a diverse panel of experts and thought leaders to share their insights and engage in a dynamic discussion on this timely topic

3

To promote environmental conservation by replanting indigenous trees in open urban spaces and on the slopes of Mt. Kenya. Together we can nurture the roots of a greener, more sustainable world for generations to come.

4

Once you experience the stories, we will invite you to book clubs, debates, workshops, plays, and concerts that give you the full patriotic experience.

Events Calender

20 October - 12 December 2023

Freedom Festival Launch: Mashujaa Day to Jamhuri Day

During this 8 week season, we will welcome audiences from all over Nairobi to come and celebrate Kenyan freedom. The event will be featured in 3 key locations; Karura Forest for Episode 1, The Karen Blixen Museum for Episode 2, and The Nairobi Gallery for Episode 3. We will also open 2 locations for the First Patriots paintball shooting experience i.e. Waterfront Karen and Two Rivers Mall, and an escape room at The Nairobi Gallery.

Expected Attendance: 20,000

February 2024

Black History Month

During the entire Black History Month, we will feature new episodes released at our key locations and expound on this by featuring debates and discussions on the links between African and African American experiences of the fight for Freedom. We will invite activists and the media from both Africa and America to partner with us on this and have the sessions organised in person and virtually as well.

Expected Attendance: 10,000

March 2024

March For Freedom

This month will highlight all the main demonstrations that were made in the history of the fight for independence, starting with the protest for the release of Harry Thuku in 1922. Harry Thuku was arrested on March 14th and protests continued all the way till the 16th, ending in the unfortunate death of Mary Muthoni Nyanjiru, a Kenyan political activist remembered for leading the protest. She was shot and killed by the police while leading this protest. We will therefore release a special episode in honour of this history and release it at the very location this demonstration occured i.e. present day Harry Thuku Road.

Expected Attendance: 8,000

May 5 2024

African World Heritage Day

As Africa celebrates its World Heritage Site, we will dedicate the whole month of May to celebrate Mt. Kenya as a UNESCO World Heritage Site. We will therefore produce an exclusive episode only available in Mt. Kenya, and organise hikes with a special tour that will help people discover more about how the freedom fighters navigated this forest and used it as their defence while they fought for against the British.

Expected Attendance: 1,000

May 25 2024

Africa Day

As we celebrate African World Heritage in Mt. Kenya, we would like to invite people to also celebrate Africa Day together with the African Union, making this a celebration of African Unity.

June 2024

Madaraka Month

On June 1, Kenya celebrates the day in 1963 when Kenya gained internal self rule. We will therefore take this entire month to tell the stories of the first Kenyan men and women who formed the first political movements and helped form the first Kenyan government. Many times, people only remember the forest fighters but through these series of episodes, we will bring to the forefront the stories of the Kenyans who built the infrastructure around African-led governments.

Expected Attendance: 10,000

20 October - 31 December 2024

Mashujaa Day to Jamhuri Day: Freedom Festival launch

One year on, we want to make this event bigger and better and have these celebrations all the way till the end of the year. We will have double the locations, double the content, and feature exciting events such as musical concerts and reenactments.

Expected Attendance: 40,000
