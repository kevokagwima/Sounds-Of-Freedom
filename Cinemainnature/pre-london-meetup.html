Why This Moment Mattered

As part of the 2025 UK/Kenya Season of Culture, this gathering was more than just a cultural event — it was a timely moment of reflection, truth-telling, and imagination. Supported by the British Council and The Africa Centre, it created space to explore the stories that have shaped us, the histories we’ve inherited, and the creative tools we now hold to reshape them.

The panel discussions — Canvas of Courage, Daughters of Freedom, Home and Away, and Purpose-Led Partnerships — were designed to confront colonial legacies, honour untold histories, and spotlight the power of art, memory, and community in shaping a more just future.

For African communities and young people especially, these conversations remain essential. They offer a mirror, a bridge, and a blueprint — affirming that storytelling is not just remembrance, but resistance and renewal.

This event also served as our Pre-London showcase — a chance to reflect on the journey of Echoes of Freedom so far and share what’s ahead for our Richmond Park exhibition and the conversations we hope to spark in the UK this Madaraka Weekend.

Together, we are not just remembering history. We are rewriting our place in it.

Key Themes

10:00 am - 10:45 am

Canvas of Courage

Art as Memory, Resistance, and Healing

Exploring how Kenyan artists use media, film, visual art, music, and performance to engage with the colonial past — challenging dominant narratives, honouring untold stories, and asserting cultural memory.

Featured Speakers:

Martin Kigondu

Actor, Director, Producer

Queen Gathoni

Creative Producer, Too Early for Birds

Maureen Anzaye-Kubai

Programme Manager, Cultural Heritage SSA, British Council

Mutana Wanjira - Moderator

Creative Producer, Founder of Sounds of Freedom Africa

10:45 am - 11:30 am

Daughters of Freedom

Women Who Fought Then, Women Who Lead Now

Spotlighting the often overlooked but critical role that African women played during Kenya's fight for independence — as warriors, strategists, mobilisers, and memory keepers.

Featured Speakers:

Wanjira Muthoni

Poet and Storyteller

Mutanu Kyany'a

Head of Programs and Outreach, African Digital Heritage

Junniah Wamaitha

Project Coordinator, Eastern African Museum of Art Nairobi

Martha Wanjiru Kamau - Moderator

Community Development Practitioner

11:45 am - 12:30 pm

Home and Away

Diaspora Voices Reflecting on Colonial Legacies

A dedicated roundtable bringing together members of the UK diaspora currently living or working in Kenya to engage in a reflective and forward-looking conversation about colonial legacies, identity, and belonging.

Featured Speakers:

Judge Peter Herbert

British Barrister and Political Activist

Julia Ekong

Founding member of DeroArts, Kisumu and Council Member, Kenya Museum Society

Mutana Wanjira - Moderator

Creative Producer, Founder of Sounds of Freedom Africa

12:30 pm - 1:00 pm

Purpose-Led Partnerships

Culture as a Catalyst for Connection

Exploring how cultural heritage and creative experiences can serve as powerful catalysts for connection, innovation, and shared purpose within organisations and between nations.

Featured Speakers:

Joyce Kanze Nzovu

Programme Manager, Arts Kenya, British Council

Dyonis Ndung’u

Regional Operations Lead, Arts SSA, British Council

People-centric consultant | Organisational Psychology & Culture | Creating lasting impact
