Why This Moment Matters

As we celebrate the 2025 UK/Kenya Season of Culture, this is more than a cultural event — it’s a timely gathering rooted in reflection, truth-telling, and imagination. Supported by the British Council and The Africa Centre, this event creates space to explore the stories that shaped us, the histories we’ve inherited, and the creative tools we now hold to reshape them.

The panel discussions — from Canvas of Courage to Daughters of Freedom, Home and Away and Purpose-Led Partnerships — are designed to confront colonial legacies, honour untold histories, and spotlight the power of art, memory, and community in shaping a more just future.

For African communities and young people especially, these conversations are essential. They offer a mirror, a bridge, and a blueprint — affirming that storytelling is not just remembrance, but resistance and renewal.

This is also our Pre-London showcase — a chance to reflect on the journey of Echoes of Freedom so far and share what’s ahead for our Richmond Park exhibition and the conversations we hope to spark in the UK this Madaraka Weekend.

Together, we’re not just remembering history. We’re rewriting our place in it.

Key Themes

10:00 am - 10:45 am

Canvas of Courage

Art as Memory, Resistance, and Healing

Exploring how Kenyan artists use media, film, visual art, music, and performance to engage with the colonial past — challenging dominant narratives, honouring untold stories, and asserting cultural memory.

Featured Speakers:

Martin Kigondu

Actor, Director, Producer

Queen Gathoni

Creative Producer, Too Early for Birds

10:45 am - 11:30 am

Daughters of Freedom

Women Who Fought Then, Women Who Lead Now

Spotlighting the often overlooked but critical role that African women played during Kenya's fight for independence — as warriors, strategists, mobilisers, and memory keepers.

Featured Speakers:

Wanjira Muthoni

Poet and Storyteller

Mutanu Kyany'a

Head of Programs and Outreach, African Digital Heritage

Junniah Wamaitha

Project Coordinator, Eastern African Museum of Art Nairobi

Martha Wanjiru - Moderator

Community Development Practitioner.

11:45 am - 12:30 pm

Home and Away

Diaspora Voices Reflecting on Colonial Legacies

A dedicated roundtable bringing together members of the UK diaspora currently living or working in Kenya to engage in a reflective and forward-looking conversation about colonial legacies, identity, and belonging.

Featured Speakers:

Judge Peter Herbert

British Barrister and Political Activist

Julia Ekong

Board Member, Kenya Museum Society

12:30 pm - 1:00 pm

Purpose-Led Partnerships

Culture as a Catalyst for Connection

Exploring how cultural heritage and creative experiences can serve as powerful catalysts for connection, innovation, and shared purpose within organisations and between nations.
